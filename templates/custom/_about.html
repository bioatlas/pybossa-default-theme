In the BioAtlas citizen science projects, we expand and develop this collaboration further, and enable collection of larger amounts of data. BioAtlas brings together various digitization projects, with the aim of improving museum collections with geographical data and link coordinates to each specimen.") }}

{{ _("Coordinate information provides important information about our museum objects. It is required for analyses of locality data that further our understanding of individual species distribution patterns and the biological diversity of fungi. Here you can help improve this knowledge by assigning coordinates to our observation records, and each contribution is an important piece of the puzzle! The coordinates will be included in our botanical database and used in further research and conservation biology monitoring programs.") }}
